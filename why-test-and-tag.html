Why it matters

Test & tag keeps people safe and your business compliant

Beyond stickers, it’s a structured approach to electrical safety, duty of care, and maintaining insurance coverage.

It’s a legal obligation

Australian workplace health and safety regulations require persons conducting a business or undertaking (PCBUs) to ensure electrical equipment is safe to use. Regular inspection by a competent person is the recognised way to meet this duty and demonstrate compliance.

Missing these checks can lead to fines and, importantly, increased risk to staff, clients, and visitors. A documented testing program is proof that you’re actively managing electrical safety.

It prevents incidents

Faulty cords, worn insulation, and malfunctioning RCDs are common causes of shocks and electrical fires. Routine testing and tagging helps you:

  • Catch unsafe equipment before it fails.
  • Protect teams, customers, and property.
  • Maintain productivity with planned maintenance.
  • Keep insurance policies valid with documented compliance.
